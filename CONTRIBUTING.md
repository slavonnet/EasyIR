# Contributing to EasyIR

Thank you for helping improve EasyIR. Please read this document before you open
a pull request.

This project is released under the terms in [`LICENSE`](LICENSE). By submitting
a pull request or otherwise contributing material to this repository, you agree
that your contribution is licensed under the same license as the project at the
time your contribution is merged, unless explicitly agreed otherwise in writing
by the maintainers.

## Grant of rights for pull requests

When you open a pull request, you grant the maintainers of EasyIR a perpetual,
worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use, reproduce, modify,
prepare derivative works of, publicly display, publicly perform, sublicense, and
distribute your contribution as part of the project, including for maintenance,
release, and promotion of EasyIR.

For clarity, this grant includes the rights needed for maintainers to incorporate
your contribution into the project and to exercise those rights on behalf of the
project under the then-current project license, consistent with typical open-source
collaboration.

You represent and warrant that you have sufficient rights to grant the license
above for everything you submit in the pull request.

## Third-party code and rights

You are responsible for ensuring your contribution does not include material you
do not have the right to submit under these terms.

In particular, you warrant that your contribution:

- does not include third-party code, assets, or excerpts you cannot license to
  the project under the project’s license and this contribution policy; and
- complies with any applicable notices, licenses, or attribution requirements
  for any third-party material you **do** include (for example: vendored code,
  copied snippets, or adapted documentation), including retaining required
  copyright and license notices where appropriate.

If you are unsure, ask in the pull request before merging.

## Attribution and forks (project policy)

Reuse and fork naming expectations for downstream users are summarized in
[`LICENSE`](LICENSE) (attribution; renamed forks after upstream inactivity).

## Development notes

- Keep pull requests focused and easy to review.
- Follow existing code style and patterns in the repository.
- When you change runtime behavior, update user-facing documentation and add or
  update tests as appropriate.
